diastolic abbreviated 120 80 mmhg traditionally blood pressure was measured non-invasively using a mercury manometer and this is still generally considered the gold standard 1 more recently other sphygmomanometers were often inaccurate but more modern validated devices have similar accuracy to mercury devices 1 blood pressure is influenced by cardiac output total peripheral resistance and arterial stiffness and varies depending on situation emotional state activity and relative health disease states in the short term it is regulated by baroreceptors which act via the brain to influence nervous and endocrine systems blood pressure that is low due to a disease state is called hypotension and pressure that is consistently high is hypertension both have many causes and may be of sudden onset or of long duration long term hypertension is a risk factor for many diseases including heart disease stroke and kidney failure long term hypertension is more common than long term hypotension long term hypertension often goes undetected because of infrequent monitoring and the absence of symptoms
